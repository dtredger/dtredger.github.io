Antipodes - github

Antipodes is an angular-based site that finds your antipode, and the closest city to there; ie: the city farthest from you.

The site takes your lat/lon from browser geolocation (or shows a slick error message), then calculates the geographic antipode, and centers a mapbox map on that location.

From that point, the site can find the closest city by comparing your anti-coordinates to a 90mb csv. The csv is a list of cities and their lat/lon, and I use the papaparse.js library to churn through them, saving the closest one. Webworkers read the csv so the browser tab doesn't just grind to a halt. There's an angular directive to show a progress bar of parsing status (it only updates every %5 done, because the more the webworkers update progress, the slower the browser gets...)

Since everything happens client-side, the site lives here on s3. It's currently only set-up to parse the first 100 lines of the csv, for testing, so it will say Abu Dabi. Other issues are:

  • Probably not a good idea to load a 90mb csv for every pageload
  • Calculation of closest city is inaccurate, since it assumes lines of latitude are the same distance apart as lines of longitude (not true), and assumes that lines of longitude are always the same distance apart (also not true). *In the North American case, I doubt it matters much because our antipode is the middle of the ocean.

piDashboard - github

piDashboard is based on rDash Dashboard.

The site is an angular-based dashboard for data provided by a Raspberry Pi (see cuddlefish/RaspberryPi).

Currently the site has two functions-ish:

  • Display a carousel of timelapse images captured by a raspberry pi.
  • Show a graph of temp/humidity recorded by a raspberry pi

A crude live version of the site is available at cuddlefish.co. It's being served by nginx running on a raspberry pi (the same one as provides the data). Very much a work-in-progress, because:

  • Carousel isn't a good way to show timelapse images (many of which are pitch-dark...), but the raspberry pi is too busy to do video-processing
  • humidity graph shows all data-points (no ability to date-filter for recency).
